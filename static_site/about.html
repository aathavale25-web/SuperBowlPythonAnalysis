About This Analysis

Methodology, data sources, and interpretation guide

Data Sources

All analysis is based on historical data from official NFL sources:

  • Super Bowl Games: Complete game data including quarter-by-quarter scores
  • Player Stats: Individual game logs from the 2024 season
  • Playoff Games: Playoff game results from 2020-2024 seasons

Methodology

Squares Analysis

The squares analysis applies recency weighting to historical Super Bowl data:

  • Last 5 years: 3x weight
  • Last 15 years: 2x weight
  • Older games: 1x weight

Probabilities are calculated assuming independence between winner and loser digits.

Player Props

Player prop analysis includes:

  • Hit Rates: Percentage of games where player exceeded the betting line
  • Best Bets: Props with >65% hit rate
  • Trends: Comparison of last 5 games vs previous 5 games

Playoff Props

Playoff analysis covers 15 games from 2020-2024 seasons:

  • Game props: Total points, winning margin, defensive TDs
  • Round-specific trends: Super Bowl, Conference, Divisional
  • Best props: Props with >60% historical hit rate

How to Interpret Hit Rates

Hit rates represent historical performance:

  • Green (>60-65%): Strong historical trend
  • Amber (40-60%): Moderate, near coin flip
  • Red (<40%): Weak historical performance

Important: Past performance does not guarantee future results. Sample sizes vary - check game counts.

Limitations

  • Historical data may not reflect current team/player performance
  • Small sample sizes (especially for playoff props)
  • Does not account for matchups, injuries, or game conditions
  • Squares probabilities assume digit independence
  • Player trends based on season averages, not opponent-adjusted

Technology

This analysis is powered by:

  • Python 3.14 with Polars for data processing
  • DuckDB for data storage
  • Plotly for interactive visualizations
  • Jinja2 for static site generation
